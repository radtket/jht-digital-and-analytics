Don Padgett

Don Padgett is a leader in the firm’s technology business, with a new focus in our civil market in the Department of Homeland Security, Department of Justice, and the Department of Transportation. With more than 30 years of experience providing management and technology services to the public sector, he helps clients achieve organizational and mission success through strategic use of business process and technology.   His expertise includes: • IT strategy • Program management • Chief Information Officer support services • Business information system implementation • Technology Insertion • Capture management and proposal development Don joined our firm in 1989 as a research analyst. He is a recognized leader in the areas of mission analysis, IT, process improvement, and change management. Don has worked with numerous civilian agencies, state governments, and all military services. He has provided IT program management, application development, knowledge management, and strategic planning consulting services across functional disciplines, including logistics, plans, acquisition, budgeting, and technology.   Prior to joining the firm, he worked as an application developer for a government organization and as an analyst for a consulting firm.   Don holds a B.B.A. in management information systems from James Madison University and an M.S. in information systems from George Mason University. He is a certified project management professional and holds a certification from Georgetown University in change management.

Arthur Mitchell

Arthur Mitchell is a Data Science and Digital Transformation thought leader with over 20 years of experience leading public-sector engagements for both Intelligence and Civil agencies. His background in statistical analysis, public policy and IT integration serve him well to bring together all aspects of Booz Allen’s broad capability set to solve our clients most challenging problems. A Principal in our Justice, Homeland Security, and Transportation (JHT) account, Mr. Mitchell is primarily focused on the Department of Homeland Security Market (DHS), responsible for developing and implementing our digital strategy across the sub-accounts. He also involved in developing new data science capabilities within the JHT account in coordination with his counterparts in the Strategic Investment Group. In all his roles, he emphasizes a collaborative environment, ensuring his teams share best practices, lessons learned, and capabilities that better connect staff and clients. Mr. Mitchell is a certified Scrum master and has multiple certifications in ITIL and has been PMP certified. He attained a BS in Political Science and MS in Public Policy Analysis from the University of Rochester.

Andy Suh

Andy is a Principal in JHT responsible for leading the US Postal Service Account and helping to grow our presence at the FTA and FRA. For the USPS, we are involved in helping the client assess the next generation of postal delivery vehicles, manage the USPS.com site, improve the Postal Service’s cybersecurity posture, and deliver enterprise wide project, program, and portfolio management support. Across FTA and FRA, we are providing a broad project, program, and grants management support. Andy is also responsible for developing our Analytics talent across JHT and specifically focusing on helping clients with fraud analytics and anomaly detection. Previously, Andy worked with financial regulatory, banking, and housing finance clients, which included the IRS, Freddie Mac, Fannie Mae, SEC, CFPB, and FDIC. Over the course of 20+ years, Andy has built a broad set of expertise in initiatives strategy, operations, organizational design, workforce optimization, employee satisfaction & productivity, human capital metrics & analysis, and HR information technology to help clients improve their operational effectiveness. Prior to returning to Booz Allen, Andy was the Director of Operations & Strategy for the Human Resources division at Freddie Mac. In that role, he was responsible for overseeing HR’s operational infrastructure and associated processes, including HRIT, Payroll, Budgeting & Planning, and operations risk management. Andy also served as the Director of Workforce Analysis at Capital One Financial. His group developed analytically grounded solutions to complex workforce issues and sought to optimize the leadership’s effectiveness in managing the firm’s human capital. Example projects included workforce optimization, cultural renewal, employee engagement, and workforce intelligence reporting via the HR Dashboard. Prior to entering the financial services industry, Andy was a management consultant in the Operations Practice of Booz Allen’s Worldwide Commercial Business specializing in manufacturing strategy and operations management, supply chain management, organization restructuring. Andy was also a nuclear plant supervisor at Bettis Atomic Power Laboratory (GOCO currently operated by Bechtel) providing operational and instructional support to the U.S. Navy’s nuclear program. Andy received an MBA from the MIT Sloan School of Management and a BS in Mechanical Engineering from Cornell University.
